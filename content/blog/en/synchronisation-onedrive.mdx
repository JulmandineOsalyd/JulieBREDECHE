Most OneDrive synchronization problems in organizations are not technical. They come from a **poor understanding of how the tool works**.

Here are 3 essential points that prevent 80% of daily incidents.

---

## 1. OneDrive Storage ≠ OneDrive App

This is the most common confusion and the one that generates the most support tickets.

### OneDrive Storage (Cloud Storage)

- Your personal storage space accessible via browser or desktop
- Files stored online in the Microsoft cloud
- Default capacity: 1 TB

### The OneDrive App (Sync Tool)

- The app installed on your PC that creates a local OneDrive folder
- Automatically syncs files from your OneDrive, SharePoint, and Teams
- Allows working offline
- **Reflects** the cloud content and doesn't store it independently

> **In short:** deleting a file from the local OneDrive folder also deletes it in the cloud. It's the same file, not a copy.

---

## 2. Adding a Shortcut ≠ Syncing

Microsoft now recommends **no longer syncing SharePoint libraries** but using shortcuts instead.

### Add a Shortcut to OneDrive (Recommended Method)

- Libraries appear in your OneDrive folder
- All cloud files accessible from one place
- Shortcuts are retained when changing computers
- Simple removal with no risk
- **Microsoft's recommended practice**

### Sync Libraries (Old Method)

- Creates a separate folder with the organization name
- Older approach
- De-syncing carries a risk of local data deletion
- Limited modern use

> ⚠️ **Important:** the same folder cannot be both synced and linked by a shortcut. Choose one method or the other.

---

## 3. OneDrive Sync Limits

OneDrive is not designed to sync unlimited volumes. Knowing its limits avoids unpleasant surprises:

| Limitation | Value |
|---|---|
| Maximum number of synced files | 300,000 files |
| Maximum full path length | 400 characters |
| Forbidden characters in file names | `" * : < > ? / \ \|` |

Beyond 300,000 synced files, performance slowdowns appear. A folder cannot be simultaneously synced and linked by a shortcut.

---

## Best Practices

- **Limit sync** to actively used folders
- **Create simple, clear folder structures** from the start
- **Prefer OneDrive shortcuts** over classic SharePoint library sync
- **Train users** on OneDrive's cloud fundamentals

---

## Conclusion

The vast majority of OneDrive problems are **educational, not technical**. Understanding the distinction between storage space and sync tool, and between shortcut and sync, is enough to resolve most incidents.

A 30-minute training session on these fundamentals can save your IT team hours of support.
