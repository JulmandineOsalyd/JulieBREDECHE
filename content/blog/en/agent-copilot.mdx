## Agent vs Chatbot: What's the Difference?

### The Chatbot: The Conversational Tool

A **chatbot** is designed to **hold a conversation**.
It answers questions, rephrases, discusses… but is often limited to text-based exchanges.

It is primarily a conversational tool built from predefined scenarios.

### The Agent: The Intelligent Business Assistant

An **agent** goes much further. It is an **intelligent business assistant** integrated at the heart of Microsoft 365.

An agent's capabilities are much broader:

1. **Answers questions:** Provides short, precise answers based on instructions and trusted sources.
2. **Guides processes:** Walks users through common tasks, step by step.
3. **Executes tasks (optional):** Can send an email on behalf of the user, for example (if actions are enabled).
4. **Operates according to instructions:** The agent follows exactly the objectives, tone, and scope defined during its configuration.
5. **Uses knowledge sources:** It draws on selected content (internal policy pages, FAQs…).

> **Note:** Declarative agents are fast to create and simple to deploy, while respecting Microsoft's security and compliance rules.

## Declarative Agents or Advanced Agents — How to Choose?

There are two main families of agents in the Microsoft ecosystem:

- **Declarative agents**: fast, no-code, based on instructions + knowledge sources.
- **Advanced agents**: richer, but require additional licenses and workflows.

Here's a summary table to make it clearer:

|  | Declarative Agent | Advanced Agent |
|---|---|---|
| **Complexity** | Light, no-code, based on instructions and sources | More complex, includes workflows and actions |
| **Licenses** | Copilot Chat license sufficient | Full Copilot Studio license |
| **Capabilities** | Answers, guides, and can execute simple actions | Can trigger forms, send confirmations, manage workflows |

## Example: An HR Agent for New Employee Onboarding

### Objective

Support each new employee from day one: understand their benefits, find the right documents, track their onboarding journey… all without constantly involving the HR team.

### Knowledge Sources Used

- The official HR policy pages on the Intranet / SharePoint
- The Onboarding FAQ available in the Teams channel dedicated to welcoming new hires
- IT guides and access procedures, centralized in a SharePoint site

### What a Declarative Agent Can Do...

- Answer the most common questions from new hires: leave, benefits, equipment, tools, processes…
- Direct users to key SharePoint documents and pages (IT charter, first-week schedule, employee handbook…)
- Cite the correct HR source to build trust and avoid misunderstandings

Ideal for reducing daily interruptions to HR teams.

### ...and What an Advanced Agent Would Add

- Automatically trigger enrollment forms useful to the employee
- Manage the entire onboarding workflow: check completed steps, send reminders, notify the manager of progress
