## What Is a Prompt?

A prompt is an **instruction** or **command** written in natural language to an AI model in order to generate a **response** or perform a **specific task**.

This response can take different forms: text, code, image, audio, or video.

## Why Master the Art of Prompting?

Mastering prompt writing allows you to:

- **Gain precision**: get more relevant and better-targeted responses
- **Gain efficiency**: accelerate your workflows and get immediate results
- **Maximize quality**: fully leverage Copilot's capabilities
- **Improve the experience**: interact more smoothly and satisfyingly

---

## The Ingredients of a Good Prompt

A good prompt draws on up to 8 key components. You don't need to include all of them every time, but the more you use, the better the response.

### 1. Context

Clarifying the situation you're in allows Copilot to adopt the right tone and register.

### 2. Role

Assigning a role or expertise to Copilot ensures more precise and professional responses.

### 3. Task

Clearly stating what you expect produces far more precise results than a vague request.

### 4. Action

Use precise action verbs: *write*, *summarize*, *answer*, *list*, *extract*… Less ambiguity, more efficiency.

### 5. Format

Specify the expected structure: bullet list, table, number of paragraphs, word count…

### 6. Tone

Express the desired style: formal, persuasive, humorous, empathetic…

### 7. Details

Include elements that may seem minor but refine the precision of the result.

### 8. Audience

Targeting a specific audience ensures relevance and appropriate vocabulary.

## Best Practices

| ✅ Do | ❌ Avoid |
|---|---|
| Be **clear and specific** in your request | Vague requests with no action verb or context |
| Provide as many details as possible | Requesting content that is unethical, confidential, or discriminatory |
| Use conversational mode and iterate to refine responses | Disrespectful or overly casual language |
| Write with correct punctuation and spelling | Contradictory instructions |
| Verify the accuracy of responses and their sources | Abruptly changing subjects mid-conversation |
| Continue an existing conversation when the context is relevant | Asking about recent events or current news |

## Some Prompting Techniques

Beyond the structure of a prompt, certain techniques can further optimize your interactions with Copilot. Here are three to know.

### Few-shot Prompting

Provide **concrete examples in your prompt** to guide Copilot toward the expected result. By showing what you want, you orient the model before it even generates its response.

**The model: ACTION + CONTENT = RESULT**

### Zero-shot Prompting

Give a **direct instruction**, with no example or additional context. Ideal for simple, fast requests, this technique relies on the clarity of the wording to get a precise and effective response.

**The model: EXAMPLE + EXPECTATION = RESULT**

### Reverse Prompting

Reverse the process: **provide a text** and ask Copilot to find the prompt that could have generated it. An excellent way to improve, find inspiration, and progressively refine how you phrase prompts.

**The model: CONTENT + EXPECTATION = PROMPT**
