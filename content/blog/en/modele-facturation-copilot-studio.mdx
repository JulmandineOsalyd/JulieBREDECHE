Since **November 1, 2025**, Microsoft has deeply restructured the billing for AI Builder and Copilot Studio. The stated goal: offer "finer, fairer billing, better aligned with actual AI usage."

Here's what this changes in practice.

---

## Copilot Credits: The New Unit of Measure

All AI actions now consume **Copilot credits**, whether triggered via agents, Copilot Studio, or the AI Builder connector.

| Action Type | Example | Unit of Measure | Consumption |
|---|---|---|---|
| Basic text AI | OCR recognition | 1 page / image / 1,000 characters | 0.1 credit |
| Standard text AI | Text translation | 1 page / image / 1,000 characters | 1.5 credits |
| Premium text AI | Multi-document synthesis | 1 page / image / 1,000 characters | 10 credits |
| Content processing tools | Invoice analysis | 1 page / image | 8 credits |

---

## The 3 Billing Models

### 💰 Pay-as-you-go

Billed on actual consumption, at **$0.01 per credit**.

Suited for testing or occasional AI usage.

### 📆 Monthly Capacity Pack

Purchase a fixed volume of credits per month.

**Example:** $200 for 25,000 monthly credits.

Ideal for regular, predictable usage.

### 💳 Prepaid Annual Plan

Annual upfront payment with **volume-based discounts**.

Pricing on request: the most economical option for intensive AI usage at organizational scale.

---

## What This Changes Concretely for Organizations

**Billing aligned with reality**

Costs are now directly correlated to actual consumption, rather than underused licenses.

**Adjustable capacity**

Credit volumes can be modified without service interruption or agent redeployment.

**Unified AI logic**

All Microsoft AI actions follow the same rate card, with real-time tracking by agent or environment.

> ⚠️ **Important note:** AI Builder credits included in Power Automate or Power Apps Premium licenses **expire on November 1, 2026**. Plan the transition now.

---

## Which Model to Choose?

There's no universal answer. The optimal model depends on:

- Your actual **AI usage volume**
- The **frequency** of processing
- The **business criticality** of your use cases

Microsoft positions AI as "a measurable, manageable, and industrializable service." This new model moves in that direction, provided volumes are properly anticipated to avoid budget surprises.
