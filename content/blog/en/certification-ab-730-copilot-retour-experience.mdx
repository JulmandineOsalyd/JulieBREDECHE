I passed the Microsoft AB-730 certification. What this certification tests is not the ability to write good prompts. It's the understanding of what happens *around* them: how Copilot accesses data, how access rights and sensitivity labels shape its responses, how it connects across Microsoft 365 apps.

Prompting accounts for 35% of the exam. The rest is governance and orchestration.

---

## What is the AB-730?

The AB-730 is officially called **Microsoft Certified: AI Business Professional**.

It's a certification designed for professionals who support Copilot deployment in organizations: M365 consultants, adoption leads, change managers, project managers. Not for technical admins or developers.

> Full exam details are available on the [official Microsoft Learn page](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/credentials/certifications/resources/study-guides/ab-730).

---

## What's covered: governance, agents, and content

### 1. Generative AI fundamentals (25-30%)

The exam tests understanding of **how Copilot accesses data**, with a focus on:

- **File access rights**: Copilot only sees what the user has permission to see. If a file stored in SharePoint isn't accessible to the user, Copilot won't index it.
- **Sensitivity labels**: Copilot respects the sensitivity labels applied to the organization's files.
- **Risks to know**: hallucinations, prompt injection, over-reliance on AI responses.

### 2. Managing prompts and conversations (35-40%)

The exam covers day-to-day prompt management: **referencing the right files, saving and scheduling a prompt, sharing it with a colleague.**

But the most interesting part is agents. The exam tests the ability to:

- Choose between an existing agent (e.g. Researcher) and building a custom one
- Configure an agent: knowledge sources, instructions, suggested prompts
- Share an agent with members of the organization

### 3. Generating and analyzing content (25-30%)

The last section covers something I often see misunderstood in the field: **how to move from one app to another with Copilot**.

- Generate a Word document from a prompt
- Move data and insights across Microsoft 365 apps
- Use Copilot Pages to collaborate on content
- Leverage Teams meeting summaries in a workflow

---

## What I take away as an M365 consultant

Beyond validating my existing skills, going through this certification helped me build **a more structured diagnostic framework**.

When a client tells me *"Copilot can't find our files"*, I now know how to structure the diagnosis:

1. Are the files accessible to the user (SharePoint permissions)?
2. Do they have a restrictive sensitivity label?
3. Are they within the scope indexed by Microsoft 365?

I would have asked the same questions before, by instinct. Now I ask them in the right order, with the certified vocabulary to explain it to the client.

> **Certification doesn't replace experience. It gives it a framework.**

---

## My conclusion

Three takeaways after passing this certification:

1. **Data governance drives everything.** Before deploying Copilot, access rights and sensitivity labels need to be properly managed.
2. **Cross-app orchestration is underestimated.** The real value of Copilot isn't in a single app. It's in the fluidity between Outlook, Teams, Word, and SharePoint.
3. **This certification strengthens my conversations with clients.** Not as a displayed credential. As proof that the right topics have been formalized.
