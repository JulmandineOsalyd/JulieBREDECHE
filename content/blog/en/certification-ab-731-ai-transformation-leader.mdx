<a href="https://aka.ms/Cert-AILeader" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">The AB-731</a> is not a certification for developers. It targets **decision-makers** who drive AI adoption within their organization. Unlike [the AB-730 (AI Business Professional)](/blog/certification-ab-730-copilot-retour-experience), it focuses on understanding **when**, **why**, and **with which tool** to use AI.

Here are the key pillars it installs in your thinking.

---

## 1. Understanding AI types and what generative AI is

The very first reflex the certification builds: distinguishing between AI families.

- **Descriptive AI**: analyzes past data to *understand* what happened ➡️ *"What happened?"*
- **Predictive AI**: anticipates future events from historical data ➡️ *"What will happen?"*
- **Prescriptive AI**: recommends actions to optimize an outcome ➡️ *"What should we do?"*
- **Generative AI**: creates *new and original* content (text, images, code, audio) ➡️ *"What can we create?"*

Generative AI is the most recent of these four families. It broke into mainstream use with ChatGPT in late 2022. It now powers the vast majority of Copilot use cases in Microsoft 365.

---

## 2. The core vocabulary of generative AI

The certification makes sure you have a solid grasp of key concepts:

| Concept | What to remember |
| --- | --- |
| **Grounding** | Anchors responses in *verified* company sources to reduce hallucinations. |
| **Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG)** | Connects a model to an external knowledge base *at query time*, without model training. This is the key concept behind Copilot. |
| **Prompt engineering** | The art of crafting precise instructions to get better responses. See my article: [Mastering the basics of prompting](/blog/guide-prompting-microsoft-copilot). |
| **Pre-trained vs. fine-tuned model** | Pre-trained is generalist (understands language). Fine-tuned is specialized for a specific domain and more costly. |

## 3. The Microsoft AI tool catalog

In my experience, this is often where things get fuzzy. With my clients, knowing how to tell apart Microsoft Foundry, Copilot Studio, and Azure AI services saves weeks of confusion.

### Development platforms

- **Microsoft Foundry**: unified platform to build, customize, evaluate, and govern end-to-end AI solutions. Requires an Azure subscription.
- **Azure Machine Learning**: train *predictive* models on historical data.

### Azure AI Services (knowing how to tell them apart is critical)

- **Azure Document Intelligence**: extract structured data from documents.
- **Azure Vision**: image analysis, defect detection. ⚠️ Does **not** allow *generating* images.
- **Azure Language**: sentiment detection, translation, personal information detection and masking.
- **Azure Speech**: transcription and analysis of audio recordings.
- **Azure AI Search**: intelligent search that understands the meaning of questions, not just keywords.

### The Copilot ecosystem

- **Microsoft 365 Copilot**: assistant embedded in M365 apps (Word, Excel, Outlook...) that draws on your company data.
- **Microsoft Copilot Studio**: build conversational agents capable of executing actions (low-code).

## 5. AI governance and adoption in the enterprise

The certification dedicates an entire section to strategy and organizational readiness. Here is what I take away from it:

- **Data comes before AI.** A poorly protected HR file will be surfaced by Copilot to the wrong person. Classifying documents and locking down access (Microsoft Purview, least privilege) is the real prerequisite.
- **A governance council, not a technical committee.** The *AI council* brings together legal, leadership, and business teams. It sets the rules and steers the direction, without managing day-to-day operations.
- **What actually blocks adoption.** Poor data quality (the number one failure cause), a lack of trust from teams, projects launched without a real use case.
- **Champions are what drives scale.** Volunteers who share their prompts and train others. More effective than any top-down training program.

---

## My conclusion

Generative AI is just one family among four. The right tool shifts depending on the need. And without data governance, no AI initiative will hold.

> **The profile in demand today is not the AI developer. It is the person who can translate Microsoft AI capabilities into business decisions.**
