I often work independently at client sites. But **that independence is earned through transparency**.

What I needed: a visible record of my activity, shared with the client, without having to produce it manually every day. Not to reassure them, but so they never had reason to doubt.

How did I automate my daily activity reporting without changing anything about the way I work? That is what this article is about.

---

## 1. The centrepiece: a SharePoint calendar

I started by creating a SharePoint list with a calendar view, shared with my client. They can see my days on site and a detailed log of my activity, day by day.

![SharePoint calendar view with daily activity events](/images/blog/daily-reporting-calendar.png)

SharePoint also lets you display the same calendar as a list. My client can **export the full history to Excel** in two clicks, directly from SharePoint. Useful at the end of an engagement to consolidate a summary or handle invoicing.

![SharePoint calendar in list view, exportable to Excel](/images/blog/daily-reporting-list-view-blog.png)



---

## 2. The trigger: every evening, the flow runs

My Power Automate flow starts on a scheduled trigger. Every evening at 8 PM.

The flow first checks whether I have any absences in my Outlook calendar. If none are recorded, an event is automatically created in the shared SharePoint calendar.

> **My flow works because I already have calendar discipline.** Beyond meetings, I also block out my working time slots in my calendar. My calendar reflects what I actually do during the day.

This is something I see constantly in my engagements: automating a process that does not yet exist does not create the process. See my article: [The 5 mistakes to avoid when automating your processes](/blog/5-erreurs-automatisation-processus).

---

## 3. AI Builder reads my emails and produces a summary

If I am present on a given day, the flow pulls from two sources:

- My **Outlook calendar events** for that day.
- The **preview of emails** I sent during the day.

This information is aggregated and sent to **AI Builder** via the "**Run a prompt**" action. I provide a structured prompt: the day's dynamic data as input, a clear instruction, and the expected output format.

<img src="/images/blog/daily-reporting-prompt-blog.png" alt="AI Builder prompt configuration inside the Power Automate flow" style={{width: '30%'}} />


The prompt looks something like this:

```
You are the reporting assistant for a Microsoft 365 consultant. Based on [dynamic content retrieved by Power Automate], write a summary of my daily activity in 5 to 6 bullet points. Use a professional and factual tone.
```
The flow then updates my event in the calendar with the summary produced by AI Builder.

*Note: AI Builder in Power Automate can also read structured PDFs. I cover that in another article if you want to go further: [Power Automate + AI Builder: automatic PDF reading](/blog/extraire-donnees-pdf-power-automate-ai-builder).*

---

## 4. How my client can use the daily reporting

The client does not have to do anything to make it work. They open the SharePoint list and can:

- View a summary of my activities for a specific day
- Browse the history across several weeks
- Export to Excel for their own tracking needs
- Share the view with other members of their team

**They do not need to email me to find out where things stand.** The answer is already there.

On my side, I have not changed anything about the way I work. I keep sending emails and maintaining my calendar. The flow reads what I do, and I save valuable time.

---

## 5. The essential prerequisites

This automation rests on two conditions:

1. **A task management process already in place.** I use my calendar to block out working time, Teams for quick communications, and email for important topics. Without that discipline, the flow has nothing to work with.

2. **A Copilot Studio licence or AI Builder credits.** AI Builder consumes credits each time the prompt runs. If you are unsure about your licensing situation, I explain the billing model in detail in this article: [Everything you need to know about the new Copilot Studio billing model](/blog/modele-facturation-copilot-studio).

## My conclusion

This flow **saves me at least 20 minutes a day**. Often more, on the days I would have forgotten to do my reporting and needed to piece it back together a few days later.

I can replicate the same setup for each of my clients. Each client has their own SharePoint calendar shared with me, and can follow my activity.

And the effect on the client relationship is real. This shared calendar addresses the client's need for visibility, without them ever having to ask for it.

> **Independence is earned, and it has to be visible**. A calendar that updates itself is proof that I am moving forward without anyone needing to prompt me.
