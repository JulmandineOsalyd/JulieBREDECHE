People often talk about **Copilot** as a single product. In reality, Microsoft now offers **three very different ways** to interact with it. And if you keep doing everything with M365 Copilot, you're missing out on the two most powerful ones.

---

## Three tools, three use cases

| | **M365 Copilot** | **Copilot Agents** | **Copilot Cowork** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **What for** | Everyday tasks | Repetitive, specialized tasks | Heavy lifting and automation |
| **What it does** | Answers, rewrites, summarizes | Executes a precise task, always the same way | Takes on a full assignment end-to-end |
| **Duration** | Seconds | Seconds to minutes | Minutes to several hours |
| **You are…** | in conversation | producing | delegating |
| **Analogy** | An expert colleague on the phone | An intern specialized on one task | An assistant you hand a file to |
| **Examples** | Summarize a document, draft an email, catch up on a Teams chat | Structure client notes, generate a standard PDF, process an intake form | Handle morning emails and draft replies, build an Excel model then the matching PowerPoint |

The "analogy" row is the one I keep in mind when explaining the difference to my clients.

---

## A concrete case: HR onboarding

Sophie, HR manager in a mid-sized company of 800 people, is welcoming three new hires the same week: a salesperson, a project manager, a developer. For each, she needs a personalized onboarding file: organization, key contacts, ongoing projects, schedule of first meetings.

Before Cowork, Copilot helped her occasionally: rewrite an email, summarize a meeting. Useful, but the bulk of the work still fell on her shoulders.

### What she does with Cowork

She formulates an objective, just like she would for a team member:

> *"For each of the three new hires joining this week, prepare a personalized onboarding file: organization, ongoing projects in their team, key people to meet, a 'who does what' guide tailored to their role. Job descriptions are in my SharePoint Recruitment 2026."*

She submits. She heads to her 9:30 meeting.

### What happens behind the scenes

Cowork builds a 7-step plan: read the job descriptions, identify the target teams, scan Teams channels for active projects and recent decisions, navigate team SharePoints, identify key contacts via emails and meetings, produce three Word files, drop them in the HR SharePoint.

What makes this possible: **Work IQ**, the intelligence layer that gives Cowork access to the full graph of Sophie's work data (Outlook, Teams, SharePoint, meetings).

### Key point: permissions

**Cowork strictly inherits the user's permissions.** If Sophie doesn't have access to a private Teams channel, Cowork doesn't either.

This is a fundamental safeguard, and it's exactly why **the quality of permissions in your tenant becomes more critical than ever**.

---

## The moment of truth: supervision

At 11:30, Sophie returns. Three files await her review. Cowork flags two points:

- *"I hesitated on the developer's target team: the job description mentions Data, but recent manager exchanges point to Platform. Can you confirm?"*
- *"I don't have access to the private Project Phoenix channel, which looks relevant for the project manager. Request access, or stick to public sources only?"*

This ability to **acknowledge its limits, ask for help, wait for validation** is what distinguishes a responsible agent from an AI acting without a safety net.

30 minutes of review, instead of the 4 to 5 hours manual preparation used to take.

---

## What changes, from a consultant's perspective

Three topics will come up in nearly every Cowork engagement:

1. **Permission quality**. Since Cowork inherits user rights, any fuzziness in SharePoint permissions or private Teams channels becomes an active operational risk. Permission audits, long postponed, become a priority.
2. **Use case scoping**. Who can use Cowork? For what? On which data? With what level of human validation?
3. **User training**. Formulating an objective rather than an instruction, reading and validating a plan, stopping an agent heading in the wrong direction: these are new skills.

---

## My conclusion

M365 Copilot, agents, Cowork: these aren't three versions of the same product, but **three levels of autonomy** we grant to AI.

The more we delegate, the more the groundwork matters: clean permissions, clear governance, users trained to supervise.

**The right reflex, before activating Cowork: make sure your house is in order.**
