**"We want a Power Apps application!"** — **"We want to automate this process!"**

I quickly understood that a Power Platform project isn't just about the technical side… it's about **clarity of the need** and **how it's framed**.

Here's how I manage my projects end to end, with a concrete use case to illustrate my different points.

> **Use case**: A client wants to develop a **travel booking application**.
> Until now, all requests were handled by email exchanges with the reservations office.

---

## 1. Understand the Need

This is, in my view, the **key step** — it's when we validate the technical feasibility of the project.

Before talking about "applications" or "automation", you need to ask the **right questions**:

- How many users?
- What data volume?
- Are users external to the organization involved?
- What Microsoft licenses will users need?

These exchanges make it possible to identify technical constraints early and **determine the project's feasibility**.

## 2. Scope and Estimate

Once technical feasibility is validated, I identify the **main features** of the application.

Each feature corresponds to a **user journey**, requiring a certain number of screens and automations.
> 1. I am an **employee** and I want to book a train trip.*
> 2. I am the **reservations office manager** and I'm processing a new booking request.*

This is when I produce the first estimate. But certain points must be properly accounted for:

- Architecture design
- User rights management
- Writing the technical-functional documentation
- Change management support

Note: Most projects I've delivered represent **20 to 30 days** of work in total.

## 3. Diagram and Validate Features

Once features are identified, I **map the associated processes** to review them and have them validated by the client.

My diagrams often make an impression: the client understands the logic, clarifies their needs, and feels heard. And I know exactly in what order to proceed.

![Simplified diagram of the new booking process](/images/blog/methode-schema-booking.svg)

For interfaces, I create **mockups directly in Power Apps**: this allows for a realistic projection, using the actual graphical components offered by Microsoft.

It's fast, concrete, and very meaningful for business teams.

## 4. Develop "Agile Style"

For me, agility is primarily about a **regular rhythm** and **client feedback**. The more available the teams are, the faster and better the project advances.

As soon as a feature is developed, I test it, present it to the client, then hand it over for them to test in turn, while I continue developing the next ones.

> Testing **creating a new booking request** by verifying that the business rules are respected, and checking the associated notifications.

This is often when new needs emerge: a forgotten use case, a rule to adjust…

In that case, we evaluate together and make decisions to stay within the initial estimate.

## 5. Test, Document, and Deploy

Before the full deployment, I always encourage my clients to **test the application as a whole**. Why not include a few pilot users!

I can help them formalize a **test script** when the business rules are complex. And I make sure that use cases with specific business rules are not overlooked!

Finally, I document everything on the go in a technical-functional document that includes:

- The business rules
- Process diagrams
- Configuration of data sources (SharePoint, Dataverse)
- Description of Power Automate and Power Apps components
- Possible actions for functional administrators

## 6. Maintain and Evolve

A Power Platform solution is designed to last in a constantly evolving Microsoft environment!

I put in place **error handling mechanisms** and **reporting** to monitor potential incidents.

I generally offer an annual maintenance package covering bug fixes and minor enhancements.

---

## In Summary

This management approach allows me to move forward in a structured and predictable way: every step is validated, every change is measured.

✅ &nbsp;Result: **Projects delivered on time, budget respected, and satisfied clients!**
