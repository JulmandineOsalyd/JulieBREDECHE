Never use em dashes (—) or en dashes (–) as punctuation in article body text, in either French or English translations.

**Why:** Julie considers these characters a marker of AI-generated writing. Their presence in a published article would undermine her credibility as a practitioner voice.

**How to apply:** When a French source uses a dash for parenthetical asides or list items, rewrite the sentence to avoid it entirely. Use a period, a comma, or restructure the sentence. Exception: article titles where a colon or dash is part of the title format itself.
